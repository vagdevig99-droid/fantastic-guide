  SLIDE 1 · AGENTIC BANKING ENGINE                    [0:00 - 1:00]
================================================================

SHOW    The slide. Point at the 5 numbered layers (left side) and
        the L1-L4 control ladder (right side).

SAY (60s):

"This is not a chatbot. It's a controlled coordination layer for
intent, action and trust.

Five layers — Customer Experience at the top, then Intent
Coordination through a front-door agent, Business Agents that each
own one outcome, Bank Capabilities accessed through those agents,
and Controls + Accountability at the base.

On the right is the control ladder — L1 Observe, L2 Prepare,
L3 Act within rules, L4 Act with judgement. Authority increases;
control never disappears.

Every action this engine produces carries five things: a level, a
reason, the data used, an approval or reversal handle, and an
audit record."

================================================================
  Q&A · PREPARED ONE-LINERS                        [13:00 - 15:00]
================================================================

Q: Is this real AI?
A: Demo runs scripted. Same UI runs Claude or Grok the moment you
   set an API key - backend in the project folder, ~250 lines.

Q: What about Servicing / RM?
A: Servicing exists in the app as the trust layer (Slide 3
   footer). RM-facing surface is Song Service vertical - separate
   build.

Q: How does it stay compliant?
A: Every response cites RBI policy inline - Customer Liability
   Framework, PMLA, Digital Lending Guidelines, MITC, FEMA LRS,
   BBPS. The control ladder makes autonomy explicit.

Q: What's the production timeline?
A: 30-60 days pilot design + 90-120 days controlled pilot + 6-12
   months to scale. Roadmap on Slide 5.

Q: What happens if Aria gets a decision wrong?
A: Three things. Disclosures attached BEFORE the action. Reversal
   available within a window for L3 + L4. Audit record on every
   step.

Q: Cost to run?
A: ~Rs1 per conversation on Claude Haiku, ~Rs0.50 on Grok 4 Fast.
   Sub-rupee for a 5-minute demo at the cheap tier.

Q: Who owns risk decisions?
A: Human owns advice and risk. Aria proposes, drafts, prepares.
   L4 is the only level where Aria acts without explicit
   per-action consent, and L4 is always reversible.

Q: How is this different from existing chat?
A: Look at the route badges in the demo - different specialists
   own different outcomes. Aria isn't a generalist; it's a
   coordinator that hands off to specialists with their own tool
   entitlements.

Q: What about hallucinations?
A: Every reasoning step is grounded in a tool call against the
   customer's real data. Tool chips are the audit of what was
   actually looked at.

Q: What changes for the bank's tech stack?
A: The agent layer sits ABOVE the existing capabilities -
   Payments, Credit, KYC, Offers, Disputes, Audit (Slide 1 layer
   4). It doesn't replace; it coordinates.

Q: Why these three solution plays?
A: Discovery work: value chain mapping -> capability mapping ->
   business-case scoring -> hotspot prioritisation. Servicing was
   #1 but sits outside Song Commerce & Sales (covered by another
   vertical).

Q: What does success look like at 90 days?
A: The value-measurement table on Slide 5 - 12 cells (3 plays x 4
   dimensions) - each measured against baseline. 90-day review
   against those cells decides what scales.
